How Threats Are Affecting the Ocean and Life

The ocean is under mounting pressure from multiple human‑driven threats that compromise its ability to sustain life. Pollution, ranging from plastics to chemical runoff, infiltrates marine ecosystems and poisons species at every level of the food chain. Rising global temperatures warm the seas, triggering coral bleaching, shifting migration patterns, and reducing biodiversity. Ocean acidification, caused by excess carbon dioxide absorption, weakens shells and reefs, undermining habitats that countless organisms depend on. Overfishing and habitat destruction further destabilize ecosystems, leaving them less resilient to change. These combined stresses ripple outward, disrupting climate regulation, threatening coastal communities, and diminishing the ocean’s role as a vital source of food, oxygen, and cultural connection for humanity.
