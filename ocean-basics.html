Ocean Basics

The ocean covers more than 70% of Earth’s surface and is essential for life on our planet. It regulates global climate by absorbing heat and distributing it through currents. The ocean is divided into distinct zones, each with unique ecosystems and species. Marine biodiversity is vast, ranging from microscopic plankton to the largest whales. Oceans also provide food, oxygen, and resources that billions of people rely on daily. Understanding ocean basics helps us appreciate its importance and the need to protect it.
